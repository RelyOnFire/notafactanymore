Bad sanitation really was associated with cholera, which helped miasma theory appear persuasive. The causal pathway, however, was contaminated water and food rather than poisonous air.

John Snow's work was important but did not single-handedly produce immediate acceptance. Filippo Pacini identified the cholera organism in 1854, and Robert Koch later isolated and studied it during the 1880s.
