Some scientists questioned Piltdown before 1953 because it conflicted with other fossil evidence. It nevertheless influenced prominent reconstructions of human evolution and remained institutionally accepted for decades.

The fraud's precise perpetrator has been investigated extensively, but identifying the forger is separate from the conclusive demonstration that the fossil itself was fake.
