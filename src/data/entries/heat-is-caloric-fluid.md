Caloric theory successfully described some heat-transfer phenomena and influenced early thermodynamics. Its replacement did not mean every equation developed under the theory became useless.

The modern microscopic account was assembled over time through the work of Rumford, Joule, Clausius, Kelvin, Maxwell, Boltzmann, and many others.
