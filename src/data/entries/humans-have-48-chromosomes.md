The correction concerned the normal diploid chromosome number. Some cells naturally differ: mature red blood cells have no nucleus, eggs and sperm contain 23 chromosomes, and cancers or chromosome disorders can produce other counts.

Tjio's result was published with Albert Levan in 1956. The improvement was not merely recounting the same images; better techniques produced less crowded chromosome spreads.
