Earlier scholars, including Ibn al-Nafis, had already challenged important parts of Galen's anatomy and described pulmonary circulation centuries before Harvey. The change was therefore not the work of one person in isolation.

Harvey could not directly observe capillaries. Marcello Malpighi's microscopic observations later supplied the small-vessel connection between arteries and veins.
