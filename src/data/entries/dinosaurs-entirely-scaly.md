The old claim is **narrowed**, not reversed into "all dinosaurs had feathers." Skin impressions demonstrate scales in many species, and feather distribution varied across the dinosaur family tree and across the body.

Feathers also evolved before powered flight and likely served functions such as insulation, display, brooding and sensory signalling.
