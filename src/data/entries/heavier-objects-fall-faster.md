In ordinary air, objects with different shapes and mass-to-area ratios can reach the ground at different times because drag matters. A compact heavy object may therefore fall faster than a feather without vindicating the old proportional rule.

The equality is also an approximation when comparing bodies massive enough to move Earth measurably or when extremely precise relativistic effects are considered.
