Humoral medicine was not a single unchanging doctrine, and physicians working within it often made careful observations. Its central causal framework, however, does not correspond to modern anatomy or disease mechanisms.

The transition away from humoral medicine was gradual. Different parts of the older system persisted after newer anatomical, physiological, and microbial explanations had emerged.
